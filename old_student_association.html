EMO Old Students Association (EMOOSA)

More than 4,500 inmates have graduated from the Edakara Muslim Orphanage, which has been working in the field of orphan care for more than 50 years. More than 500 people, both teaching and non-teaching staff served the institution. For years, a committee called the EMO Old Students' Association (EMOOSA) has been working systematically for their welfare. The Association is registered as MPM / CA / 470/2020 on November 04, 2020, under the Societies Act. The success of this association is since there are many EMO alumni who hold high positions in the government sector and in the Gulf countries. An EMO Old Student Welfare Fund has also been running for five years under the association. The scheme is based on the realization that the families of some of the alumni have died, others have fallen ill, and their families were suffering from severe financial hardship. The project also helped hundreds of faculty and non-teaching staff who served at EMO. The fund raises capital from alumni and staff members at the rate of Rs.100 / - per month and Rs.5000/ - per annum from expatriates and government employees. The Welfare Fund has disbursed assistance of about `1 crore over a period of five years. The association has also developed a family safety plan aimed at the continuation of voluntary social work.

Family Safety Plan

The idea behind Family Safety Plan is to maintaining the EMO Alumni Welfare Fund with the active cooperation of its members to help its members in the event of an accident. The project also aims to streamline the organization of the Old Students' Association (EMOOSA). Please see the brochure for further details of the project.
