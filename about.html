Edakkara Muslim Orphanage (EMO)

Edakara Muslim Orphanage (EMO) started in 1969 to give shelter to nine orphans in a rented building at Edakara Palathingal. It is because of the vision of Haji C Jamaluddin Muslyar and Kalathingal Hamza Haji that it became the leading educational complex in East Ernad with institutions from LKG to Plus Two. Hamza Haji was the General Secretary of the EMO for 46 years. The late Haji C Jamaluddin Muslyar also held the position of President of the Institute for a long time. At present, Markazul Uloom Madrasa, MOLP School, MOUP School, EMO Residential Girls' Higher Secondary School, Orphanage English Medium School Palad are the institutions running under EMO. The firm's permanent income is only the mere revenue from the rental collection of the Orphanage Complex which was built by the Saudi Committee of EMO on Edakkara Kovilakam Road. Yet, many community well-wishers have also helped this great institution to this date and EMO is certain that they will continue to extend their support. Today, 115 boys and girls are studying in the institution by enjoying all the facilities. These students also include those who are pursuing undergraduate and postgraduate courses and professional courses. EMO has some dreams remaining to come true like a high school in Edakkara Munda, Palad Girls' High School, Arts and Science College, boarding madrassa, Wafi Wafiyah courses, and a hospital. May Allah bless ...! Ameen.

EMO, Looking back to history

Edakara Muslim Orphanage (EMO) was found in 1969 which was a time when our area was reeling under the impact of poverty and hunger, many parents used to send their children to orphanages with the aim of getting their children daily food. The idea of starting an orphanage in Edakara was born out of a chat between Unni Hasan, Kunhahammad Mesthiri Vazhikkadavu, and Puthiyara Unni Alavi Sahib Moothedam, who came to Edakara from Nilambur to sell clothes. By the end of 1968, the formalities for this had begun. In June 1969, it started operations in a rented building at Palathingal. After the religious sermon held in Edakara to raise funds for the orphanage, only Rs. 13 was left after meeting the expenses. However, many offered land, money, and paddy every year. Thus, in 1969, nine orphans were accommodated in a rented house in Palathingal. The orphanage started its functioning. The main income was paddy and the paddy provided by the farmers in two fat pots a year. Unniyalavi Sahib of Puthiyara promised to donate six acres of paddy land and Unnihassan Sahib promised to give the children two pairs of clothes for each of the two Eid festivals as long as his cloth trade persisted. The orphanage constructed its first building on 75 cents of land provided free of cost by Kunhi Ahmed Mesthiri at Palathingal. At the end of 1969, the orphanage began trying to build its own building. In 1970, MES President Dr. Under the presidency of Abdul Ghafoor, Pookoya Thangal laid the foundation stone for the building. The work progressed rapidly. The work started from nothing and completed in 1971. The building was inaugurated by the then Education Minister CH Muhammad Koya Sahib under the chairmanship of Syed PMSA Pookoya Thangal. Moidhu, who was sentenced to death in 1977 in Mullurkara, Thrissur district, his last wish was to have her three daughters and a very young son adopted by any charity institution, the newspaper Chandrika reported. After reading the news, the then Secretary Kalathingal Hamza Haji informed President Jamaluddin Musliyar and the next day, PK Sulaiman, who was the honorary manager of EMO, went to Mullurkara, Thrissur, and took the children with them. Hereafter, the girls' wing of EMO also came into existence.
